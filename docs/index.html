⚡ ANALOG WATCHDOG CIRCUIT SIMULATOR

Hardware watchdog with MCU crash detection & automatic shutdown relay (10 Hz pulse monitor)

MCU RUNNING
How It Works
1
MCU PULSE

MCU toggles GPIO at 10 Hz. Each pulse drives Q12 transistor ON, charging capacitor C28 to ~4.5V through R66.

2
RC CHARGE

Cap charges fast on each pulse. Between pulses it bleeds through R64 (150kΩ) with τ ≈ 0.5s. At 10 Hz, cap stays ~4.5V (charged).

3
COMPARATOR

IC4 compares Cap (4.5V alive) vs V_ref (2.5V). When Cap > V_ref → IC4 HIGH → relay energised.

4
RELAY DRIVER

Q14 transistor amplifies IC4 output, pulling relay K1 coil. NC contacts stay closed while energised.

5
MCU CRASH

GPIO stops pulsing → cap drains below 2.5V in ~0.5-1.5s → IC4 flips LOW → relay opens → SHUTDOWN

6
FEEDBACK

Optocoupler TCMT1103 isolates relay state back to MCU. Firmware monitors its own watchdog health.
